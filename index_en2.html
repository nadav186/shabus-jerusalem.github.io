Who are we?

The Cooperative Transportation Association of Jerusalem was founded by Jerusalemites, young and old, who live in and love their city -- and want to make it a better place to be. The idea of founding a cooperative emerged from the growing gap between the blossoming of the entertainment and cultural activities offered on Saturday in the city and the frustrating lack of accessibility to them, due to the lack of public transportation. We want to change the current situation in which only people with their own cars or the resources to depend on taxis are able to enjoy their weekends. We are concerned that since there is no public transportation available young people often drive after drinking. We aim to make Jerusalem a more fair, fun, inclusive, and safer place on the weekends

Why do we need a cooperative?

Normally, public transportation is offered as an inexpensive way to travel. Unfortunately, despite widespread demand, the Ministry of Transportation refuses to provide public transportation in Jerusalem on Saturdays. Our solution is to create private transportation on a cooperative basis that will allow us to keep costs low, for members only. The more people that join the cooperative, the wider the range of our service will be and the more frequently the transportation will run.

Route and Timetable

Once we've reached our initial fundraising goal, we'll launch the first route of Shabus that will follow a route from Pizgat Zeev through Musrara, the city center, the German Colony and Talpiot to Gilo. The line will run between Friday nights at 8 p.m. to Saturday morning 1 a.m. at a frequency of at least one run per hour (depending on the funds we raise and how many people join us!).

If you want more routes opened or more frequent rides - tell us and contribute !

The routes and frequency may change depending on the decisions of the members of the cooperative.
